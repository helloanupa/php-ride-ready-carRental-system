5 Must-Know Car Rental Policies Before Your Next Trip

Your guide to a smooth and stress-free car rental experience.

Posted on September 25, 2024

1. Fuel Policy

What You Need to Know: Fuel policies vary between rental companies, and it’s important to clarify what’s expected. The most common policies include: Full-to-Full: You pick up the car with a full tank of gas and are required to return it full. Full-to-Empty: You pay for a full tank of fuel upfront and can return the car with any amount of fuel. Why It Matters: Returning a car with less fuel than required under a full-to-full policy may result in hefty refueling charges.

2. Mileage Limits

What You Need to Know: Some car rentals include unlimited mileage, allowing you to drive as far as you need. Others impose a daily mileage limit, and exceeding this limit can lead to additional fees. Why It Matters: If you’re planning a long road trip, unlimited mileage can save you money. Be sure to ask about mileage restrictions before finalizing your booking.

3.Additional Driver Fees

What You Need to Know: Many car rental companies charge extra fees for additional drivers. Even if it’s a family member or friend, adding another driver to the rental agreement can increase the rental cost. Why It Matters: Always check the cost of additional drivers and make sure to officially list anyone who will drive the car. Failing to do so may void the insurance coverage in case of an accident.

4. Insurance Coverage

What You Need to Know: Rental companies offer various insurance packages, from collision damage waivers (CDW) to liability insurance. While some coverage may be included, others are optional but strongly recommended. Why It Matters: Understanding what each insurance policy covers can help you avoid unexpected costs in case of damage or an accident. Check if your personal car insurance or credit card provides rental coverage to avoid paying for duplicate coverage.
